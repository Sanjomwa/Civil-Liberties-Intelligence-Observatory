# ADR-0006: The Pressure-Attribution Decomposition

Status: Accepted, implemented and verified against live data
Date: 2026-07-06

## Context

This is the build the project's own reasoning has pointed at from two independent directions, both recorded in `decision-log.md` (2026-07-05): the architecture-first analysis and the users-derived-backward analysis each independently concluded that CLIO's two real users — an investigative journalist and an NGO/human-rights-documentation buyer — need an **attributed breakdown of why pressure is elevated, not a single composite score**. It is also Recommendation #4 of the Project Zero Review ("Build the pressure-attribution decomposition… Impact: high. Difficulty: low-medium. Commercial value: high. Risk: low."), until now unbuilt. Before this change, the dashboard showed the blended `composite_pressure_score` and, separately, the regime classification; nothing decomposed *why* on a given date.

## What direct investigation established before any code was written

Per this project's standing verify-the-shape-first discipline, the inventory pass corrected two assumptions the task framing itself carried:

1. **OONI is not an arithmetic driver of the composite.** `composite_pressure_score = conflict_pressure_score*0.75 + platform_pressure_score*0.25` (ADR-0004), and `platform_pressure_score` is **Google Transparency** data (`int.google_pressure_periodized`), not OONI. OONI feeds the protocol marts and the correlation mart, but not one term of the headline score. Presenting per-app/per-protocol OONI signals as "the drivers of platform pressure" would have been exactly the category of fabricated attribution this project's anti-fabrication rule exists to prevent. The design therefore presents OONI as what it actually is: **independent, same-day, network-layer corroboration** — evidence that can support or fail to support a reading, but never moves the score.
2. **Neither composite term is day-resolved.** The conflict term is a weekly ACLED aggregate broadcast across its Saturday-anchored week (documented at TD-40's fix); the platform term is a **semiannual** Google reporting period broadcast across ~6 months (`int.google_pressure_periodized`). Consequently `composite_pressure_score` is a weekly step function within any half-year period, and *within* a week it cannot move at all. An honest attribution page must state this, not paper over it — and it also means week-over-week movement is attributable *exactly* (the platform term's delta is zero except at period boundaries).

Also confirmed: the citable detail the users need genuinely exists. `int.acled_event_classification` carries per-week × admin1 × event_type × sub_event_type rows with `pressure_domain`, `is_suppression_marker`, `severity_tier`, `classification_confidence`, `methodology_risk_level`, and a plain-language `classification_note`; `stg.google_transparency_detailed` carries product × reason per period; `marts.fact_ooni_censorship_signals` carries per-experiment daily rows with `test_name`, `protocol`, and `confidence_score` bucketable via `marts.dim_censorship_confidence` (ADR-0001). No confidence figure anywhere in this build is newly invented.

## Decision

Four new **leaf** reporting assets — each at its natural grain, never joined across grains (the TD-02 lesson, applied at design time rather than as a later fix) — plus one Streamlit page. No generalized N-source attribution framework: the decomposition names exactly the two arithmetic terms that exist, plus two differently-shaped evidence layers, clearly labeled as such.

1. **`reporting.mart_pressure_attribution_daily`** (1 row/date): each term's score, ADR-0004 weight, contribution, share of composite; the term's real grain marker; the evidence-pointer join keys (`conflict_week_start_date`, `platform_period_start/end`); the regime overlay passthrough; week-over-week deltas per term; and `attribution_residual` (composite minus the sum of contributions — a live drift alarm on the duplicated weights, rounding-level by construction).
2. **`reporting.mart_pressure_attribution_conflict_drivers`** (ACLED's own weekly-aggregate grain, rows preserved verbatim, never re-aggregated): the classified ACLED rows behind a week's conflict input, each with its KCLIO confidence/risk qualifiers and `weekly_intensity_share` — a share of the week's **pre-log intensity mass** (`events + 3*fatalities`), deliberately *not* a share of the log-scale score, because the LOG is nonlinear and a per-row share of the score itself would be fabricated precision. Shares of the linear argument are exact.
3. **`reporting.mart_pressure_attribution_platform_drivers`** (semiannual period × product × reason): Google's own breakdown with `period_detailed_share` (same pre-log share honesty; `google_requests`, the other linear input, has no product/reason breakdown in the source and is surfaced only as a period scalar).
4. **`reporting.mart_pressure_attribution_ooni_daily`** (date × test_name × protocol): daily counterpart to TD-51's monthly `fact_protocol_blocking_summary`, same `dim_censorship_confidence` join pattern, prominently labeled **corroboration, not a composite input**. `test_name` is a grouping key precisely so dnscheck's structurally zero-signal volume (TD-55) stays in its own rows — TD-49's never-pool-test-families requirement satisfied by grain, with per-test rates only.

The Streamlit page (`9_Pressure_Attribution.py`) assembles, for any selected date: the headline (composite, level, regime chip), the two-term arithmetic split with grain caveats, the week-over-week movement attribution sentence, the top conflict drivers for the containing week (with confidence/risk/suppression columns and classification notes), the platform breakdown for the containing period (with the semiannual-grain warning stated bluntly), and the OONI corroboration table for the exact date.

**What this deliberately is not:** actor-level attribution. The Zero Review flags entity resolution (`dim_entities`, TD-10) as the blocker for *actor-level* attribution — who did the pressuring — and the aggregated ACLED export contains no actor fields at all (`int.acled_event_classification` Principle 1). This build is source/term-level attribution — *what evidence, of what kind, at what confidence, drove the number* — which requires no entity resolution and whose actual prerequisites (TD-01's provenance flag, ADR-0001's confidence unification, ADR-0004's benching of the fabricated legal term) were all already resolved. Roadmap Step 6 remains the actor-level follow-on, unblocked-by and unrelated-to this change.

## Verification (all against live data, 2026-07-06)

- `bruin validate`: 60/60 assets. Each new asset materialized individually (`bruin run <asset>`, never a pipeline-wide run — the TD-54/TD-49 hold assets were not touched; staleness check re-run after: 73 edges, 0 new violations).
- Arithmetic: max `|attribution_residual|` = 0.0001 across all 761 dates; max mismatch between `composite_delta_7d` and the sum of term deltas = 0.0002 (rounding); weekly intensity shares sum to 1 in every week; platform period stamped on all 761 dates; weekly conflict totals agree exactly (0 mismatched weeks) between the daily mart (built from staging) and the drivers mart (built from `int.acled_event_classification`).
- **Finance Bill 2024 window, through the actual rendered page** (headless AppTest, zero tracebacks, both default and 2024-06-25 renders): on June 25 (Parliament stormed), composite 5.10 ELEVATED, conflict share **85.6%**, regime **CRISIS** (HIGH confidence); the week-over-week jump of +0.84 attributes **100% to the conflict term** (platform delta 0.0, as the grain analysis predicts); the week's #1 driver is **Riots/Violent demonstration, Nairobi — 4 events, 16 fatalities** (severity HIGH, classification confidence HIGH, methodology risk LOW), with the Nairobi abduction wave (7 events) visible in the same table; and OONI same-day corroboration shows **Signal, DNS layer: 177 blocking signals, all high-confidence, 8.75% of Signal's own measurements** — with dnscheck sitting in its own row at rate 0.0, diluting nothing. Every element of what is already known to be true about that window appears, from real fields, with nothing incoherent.
- TD-08 golden-file tests re-run: 6 passed (unaffected, as expected — nothing here touches ACLED's classification chain).

## Consequences

- The two users' core question now has a citable, attributed answer per date, assembled entirely from confidence fields that already existed. The finding for June 25, 2024 reads as: *elevated pressure, 85.6% conflict-driven, led by riots in Nairobi with 16 fatalities at HIGH classification confidence, regime CRISIS, corroborated same-day by high-confidence DNS interference against Signal.* That is Recommendation #4, functioning.
- Two keep-in-sync duplications were accepted deliberately and are tracked as **TD-57**: the ADR-0004 weights appear in `fact_country_pressure_daily` and `mart_pressure_attribution_daily` (mitigated by the `attribution_residual` live alarm), and the Google periodization CASE/LEAD logic now exists in three sites (`int.google_pressure_periodized` and both new marts that need period anchors). Each site carries a keep-in-sync comment naming the others.
- The pre-log share convention (shares of the LOG argument, never of the score) is now this project's stated pattern for attributing any log-scale metric — reuse it rather than inventing per-row score shares.
- The page's honesty statements (weekly step function; semiannual platform broadcast; OONI-is-not-a-composite-input) double as latent-bug tripwires: if day-to-day within-week movement ever appears, something upstream broke.

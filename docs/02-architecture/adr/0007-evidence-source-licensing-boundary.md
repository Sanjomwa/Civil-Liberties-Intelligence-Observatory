ADR-0007: Evidence Source Licensing Boundary and Commercial-Use Firewall
Status: Accepted
Date: 2026-07-11
Context
A 2026-07-11 internal licensing and compliance review (maintained as a private governance document, not published in this repository, since it discusses commercial strategy alongside the licensing findings) re-verified each evidence source's terms directly from primary sources rather than from assumption. Key findings, summarized here so this ADR is self-contained:
OONI: licensed CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 (Attribution-NonCommercial-ShareAlike), confirmed from OONI's own license repository and its official Data Policy page. NonCommercial use only; ShareAlike binds any adapted/derivative work to the same terms.
ACLED: a contractual (not Creative Commons) license — free for non-commercial use with registration, a separate paid Commercial License Agreement required for commercial use — with a specific, broad prohibition on creating a "functional substitute" for ACLED's own products or using ACLED data to train/develop AI or ML systems "regardless of whether such use is commercial, academic, or experimental."
STOP / Access Now (#KeepItOn): licensed CC BY 4.0 — permissive, commercial use and derivatives both allowed with attribution. The one source with no NonCommercial restriction.
CPJ: licensed CC BY-NC-ND 4.0 (Attribution-NonCommercial-NoDerivatives) — the most restrictive of the five; NoDerivatives forbids adapted/transformed redistribution outright, independent of the commercial question.
Google Transparency Report: no source-specific reuse or redistribution license could be located despite direct search; status recorded as undetermined, not compliant.
ADR-0003 adopted OONI and ACLED as CLIO's foundation datasets on methodological grounds alone (independent provenance, grain fit, attribution power) and never evaluated their licensing terms — this ADR closes that gap. It does not revise ADR-0003's methodological conclusion; the two datasets remain methodologically correct choices, they are now additionally understood to be commercially restricted ones.
A dedicated advisory consultation, obtained specifically for this decision, concluded that CLIO's "never redistribute third-party datasets, only transform into attributed intelligence" architectural instinct is good practice but does not, by itself, resolve either restriction — both the NonCommercial condition and the functional-substitute prohibition attach to the use of the evidence, not only to its redistribution. Transforming OONI data into an "Adapted Material" finding does not release it from NonCommercial terms; ACLED's restriction is contractual and applies regardless of how transformed the output is.
A subsequent internal strategic review (private planning documents, not published in this repository) converged on a working position: CLIO is the flagship research and methodology observatory, not the commercial product itself; grants are the primary funding mechanism for its first stage; the Finance Bill 2024 report will not be sold; and the durable commercial opportunity, if pursued later, comes from the methodology and engineering discipline, not from reselling OONI- or ACLED-derived outputs.
Decision
CLIO's OONI- and ACLED-derived intelligence layer is treated as non-commercial and grant/public-interest-funded for the foreseeable term, not as a product for direct sale. This is not presented as a permanent legal conclusion — it is the correct default under a genuinely unresolved legal question (see Consequences), adopted because it is the safer posture, because grants are the chosen funding mechanism for this stage regardless of the licensing question, and because it matches CLIO's own stated identity as a research flagship rather than a commercial product.
STOP/Access Now-derived findings are not subject to this restriction and may be used commercially once STOP is actually ingested, per ADR-0003's existing recommendation.
A single, small, disclosed, non-recurring engagement — as distinct from a scaled or subscription-style commercial product — is treated as a genuinely open question, not a concluded prohibition. The internal review found the evidence base should be non-commercial "unless and until" resolved; it did not find that one attributed, properly-cited, cost-recovery engagement is definitely a violation. This ADR does not authorize such an engagement; it records that the question has not been closed by legal counsel and should not be treated as closed in future planning.
The long-term commercial opportunity is scoped to what is licensing-independent: the classification and attribution methodology, the confidence-scoring framework, the Evidence Source Acceptance Framework (see ADR-0008), and the AI-layer discipline (Intelligence Translation Layer, Prompt Contract Layer, hard prohibitions on raw-data-to-LLM access and unsupported causal language). No implementation of any commercial spinoff is authorized by this ADR; it only records that this is the direction future commercial exploration should take if and when it resumes.
Consequences
CLIO's roadmap priority shifts from commercialization engineering (an API, a subscription tier, scaled report production) to grant-readiness engineering: compliance verification (attribution correctness on public-facing dashboard pages — see TD-63), a methodology whitepaper, and a flagship-report portfolio beyond the single Kenya case.
Provider outreach to ACLED and OONI remains valuable regardless of this ADR's non-commercial default — a future negotiated license would not require reversing this decision, only updating it, and that outreach is scheduled independently of whether it succeeds.
This ADR should be revisited if any of the following change: ACLED or OONI grant a specific commercial license or partnership; actual legal counsel resolves the "single small engagement" question either way; or STOP's ingestion (not yet built) reaches production, at which point a STOP-only commercial tier becomes possible without touching this ADR's restriction on OONI/ACLED at all.
